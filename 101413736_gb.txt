 Established in 1967, the college is named after George Brown, a prominent 19th-century Canadian politician and newspaper publisher
 The college has several campuses across downtown Toronto, including the St. James Campus, Casa Loma Campus, Waterfront Campus, and the Daphne Cockwell Centre for Health Sciences.
 George Brown College offers a wide range of programs including certificates, diplomas, postgraduate certificates, and degree programs in areas such as business, health sciences, community services, culinary arts, hospitality, design, engineering technologies, construction, and media studies
The college has partnerships with various industry leaders, allowing students to gain practical experience through internships, co-op placements, and applied research projects. 
The college boasts state-of-the-art facilities including labs, studios, and simulation centers tailored to specific programs.
